# HS-100-03 — The thesis

- **Project:** holdspeak
- **Phase:** 100
- **Status:** backlog
- **Depends on:** HS-100-02
- **Unblocks:** HS-100-04

## Problem

Grounding says what the product is for; judgment says what today's UI
does to those jobs. The thesis is the opinion the owner asked for:
what the application layer of the Desk OS SHOULD be.

## Scope

- In:
  - **docs/internal/APPLICATION_LAYER_THESIS.md**: the few real
    applications (likely fewer than today's fourteen windows) and
    what each is FOR; the information architecture per app; the
    interaction model (how a person moves between world, windows,
    and voice); the design-language commitments carried from the
    materials spike (what survives, what changes); the density,
    type, and layout rules per app archetype;
  - real MOCKUPS of the core screens (built in-product on a branch or
    as high-fidelity HTML — whichever shows truthfully), screenshot
    at 1440 and 393;
  - the delivery plan: the build stories that would execute the
    thesis, with their mechanical guards named.
- Out:
  - shipping any of it to main (the gate decides).

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] The thesis names the applications, their jobs (cited to the
      grounding), and what dies/merges from today's inventory.
- [ ] Mockups exist for every core screen at both form factors and
      are LOOKED AT — no unmocked application ships in the plan.
- [ ] The build plan is story-granular with guards named.

## Test plan

- Doc guards; mockup inventory census.

## Evidence required

- The thesis; the mockups; the plan.
